The Great Gatsby

Setting

  • Locations
    • East Egg, wealthy old money enclave on Long Island
    • West Egg, wealthy new money enclave eon Long Island
    • Valley of Ashes - gray place between New York City and the eggs - where the eye billboard is
  • 1920’s
    • Jazz Age
    • Free-spirited and fast pace
    • Focus = have fun
    • Growing economy and moving to cities
    • More freedom, time, and disposable income
    • Normalcy needed because the US had lost thousands fighting in the Great War and terrible riots and strikes were going on
    • Women began to be more politically and socially active
    • Luxuries out of reach for most Americans; middle and lower classes suffered greatly
    • During prohibition

Symbols

  • Valley of Ashes
    • Waste of capitalism
    • Consequences of the pursuit of wealth
    • Disconnect between poor and the rich
  • Green Light
    • Green often symbolic of money
    • Gatsby reaching for the money (and Daisy’s love)
    • Light comes from the end of the Buchanan's dock
    • Power of our dreams
  • Eyes of Dr. TJ Eckleburg
    • Faded billboard in Valley of Ashes
    • 2 eyes in yellow spectacles
    • “Eyes of God”
    • Comparison to Myrtle’s jealous eyes
    • Myrtle can't fool God
    • Loss of spirituality in American society
  • Owl eyes
    • The man who saw through the facade with the books in the beginning
    • Found value within gatsby
  • Breaking of clock
    • Gatsby attempt to rewind time

Themes

  • Class
    • Midwest vs East
    • New Money vs Old Money
    • Unsuccessful party trying to bring them together
  • Superficiality vs Truth
    • The double lives of the character; their outward display vs their inner self
    • Gatsby offers nick a side job in return for the tea he set up, shows lack of Gatsby true friends and need to bribe
    • Reality is never as beautiful as the truth
    • Gatsby whole life was set to impress Daisy and dresses in finery to see her, although she is not his
    • Although Gatsby had grand parties that everyone welcomed themself to, he did not have many true friends that cared enough about his death
  • The American Dream
    • What extremes will people go to in order to get it?
    • Reaching for the green light
    • Constantly wanting more, greed
  • Degradation of Society // corruption
    • Represented in the later part of the novel where characters are bitter towards one another
    • Poor becoming collateral damage

Characters

  • Nick Carraway
    • Narrator
    • Taught not to judge
    • participated in Great War
    • Went East seeking adventure and learn about business in 1922 - bond trade
    • Literary inclined / reflective / honest
    • Looks over others dishonesty
    • Consistently questions Gatsby’s possible facade
    • Lived in West Egg
    • Becomes close friend of Gatsby
    • Becomes disgusted with the pettiness
    • Ends relationship with Jordan
    • Finds body of Gatsby
    • Angered by superficiality of Gatsby’s so-called friends
    • Accused Tom of telling George Wilson that Gatsby killed Myrtle
    • Does not lose morality and moves to midwest
  • Jay Gatsby
    • Upbringing
      • James Gatz
      • Parents: poor farmers
      • Fished on lake superior where wealthy yacht owner, Dan Cody, took a liking to him because he warned him of a storm, and hired him as an assistant
      • Man of war
    • Responsive, unique
    • Started poor (poor when met Daisy)
    • Weekly parties
    • Many rumors about him / humble facade
    • Became rich by bootlegging alcohol - murky
    • Everything of his life had been for his love, Daisy, she promised to wait for him through war but didn’t - he was originally unable to marry her because her parents disapproved because he was poor
    • False belief that money can buy him anything
    • Takes blame for the death of Myrtle for Daisy’s sake
    • Refuses to leave Daisy and leads to his death
    • Killed by Myrtle husband (George)
    • Funeral continues to spread rumors
    • Father spoke highly of Gatsby
    • Never found own true dream
  • Tom Buchanan
    • Husband of Daisy
    • Went to college with Nick
    • Extremely wealthy
    • Racist / insensitive / arrogant / abusive / childish / self-involved
    • Affair with myrtle
    • Introduces Myrtle to Nick
    • Becomes jealous of Daisy’s attention for Gatsby
    • Makes many scenes about Gatsby and Daisy’s love affair and past
    • Devastated to see that Myrtle was killed
    • Tells George Wilson that Gatsby killed Myrtle
    • Abandoned home with no clue of where they are
    • No remorse
    • Admits to being distraught over death of Myrtle, but only because he had to give up his New York apartment
  • Daisy Buchanan
    • Nick’s cousin
    • Comes from money in East Egg
    • Extremely self-involved
    • “The best thing a girl can be in this world, a beautiful little fool”
    • Wealth and status trump love - if she waited for Gatsby she could have had love and wealth
    • Daisy cries after seeing Gatsby’s fine shirts
    • Affair with Gatsby
    • Kills myrtle driving Gatsby’s car (intentionally) - proving commitment to Tom
    • Tom and Gatsby love battle over her, ultimately choosing Tom- uses Gatsby to get back at Tom for his affair with Myrtle
    • Continues life without care
    • Abandoned home with no clue of where they are
  • Jordan Baker
    • Nick met her at Tom Buchannon house
    • Professional golfer
    • dishonest
    • Nick’s girlfriend
  • Myrtle Wilson
    • Tom mistress
    • Superficial / longs for attention / conform to stereotypical wealth personality (puppy)
    • Killed by Gatsby’s car, driven by Daisy
  • George Wilson
    • Husband of Myrtle
    • Owns a garage - content
    • Found out wife having an affair when falling ill
    • Believes that Myrtle death was murder
    • Gods revenge to kill Gatsby - distorted reality
    • Killed Gatsby then committed suicide
  • Mr. and Mrs. McKee
    • Downstairs apartment under Myrtle and Tom
  • Catherine
    • Myrtle sister
  • Meyer Wolfsheim
    • Gatsby’s business partner
    • Questionable past
    • gambler
