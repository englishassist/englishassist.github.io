Moby Dick Notes

Background Information

  • Moby Dick = The Whale
  • Much focus on etymology: the study of the origin of words and the way in which their meanings have changed throughout history
  • Extracts: novel begins with excerpts and quotations from other texts, highlights the danger of whale hunting
  • Time Period: between 1850-1851
  • Setting
    • begins in Massachusetts (New Bedford and Nantucket island)- the chief centers of the American Whaling Industry
    • setting shifts to the whaling ship Pequod as it cruises around the world
  • Pairings
    • pairings of mates to harpooners represent the oppression in the 19th century represents the larger social structures
  • Themes
    • brotherhood of man-at sea, practical concerns push religious matters aside skills trump race at sea
  • Gam
    • Pequod encounters with other ships

Gams

  • Gam 1- The Goney/Albatross
    • What is an albatross? A huge white bird that is stereotypically a sign of good and supernatural (white and supernatural like Moby Dick)
    • blood-rust-decay
    • At sea for 4 years
    • Ahab question if Moby Dick seen but due to wind unable to hear answer
    • Horn falls into water when Ahab asks if the Albatross has seen Moby Dick- wind keeping them from communicating
    • Fish that followed Pequod now follow Albatross saddening Ahab
    • Nature rejecting the Pequod mission- warning
  • Gam 2- Town-Ho
      • Ishmael told that story that he heard from Tashtego Steel Kilt v Radney- rivals
    • Radney: authority assigned, ranked, skillful; abusive authority
      • Threatened by Steel Kilt’s power
      • Uses his type of authority to regain his sense of positioning
      • Demands Steel Kilt to clean up poop when its not his assigned job
      • Radney comes at Steel Kilt with a hammer
      • Radney hurts Steelkilt when tied to front
      • Ships lowered for Moby Dick and Radney gets killed by him
      • Moby Dick provides divine justice
    • Steel Kilt: authority of likeability; nobility; fair-minded; leads by example; easily angered at being undermined
      • Demonstrates type of person you want to hire
      • Good internal sense of leadership
      • Steel Kilt draws line at the insult of shoveling poop
      • Says do not come closer to me with hammer-hits Radney in jaw
      • Followers in hold picking sides, Steelkilt demands they come out and they will not be flogged - if order breaks down, death will come
      • Steelkilt tied at the top of the ship and supposed to be hurt by the captain, but Radney ends up hurting him
      • Plans to kill Radney
    • Commentary of Ishmael: authority comes from internal aspect of care
  • Gam 3- Jeroboam
    • Epidemic on board the Jeroboam does not get close to Pequod. Crazy prophet on ship, Gabriel, convinced the crew that his power caused the epidemic- uses this for control
    • Archangel Gabriel is prophet of doom and fortune (delivers messages)
    • Captain thinks man is crazy, but at the same time scared to take him down
    • Man named Macy died when challenging Moby Dick
    • Gabriel says to Ahab to keep the letter for Macy because he is going to join him- on their journey to death
    • Scaramouche - the fool of all archetypal characters walk on line between the world we live in and another world
  • Gam 4- Jungfrau (virgin)
    • Out of oil- odd for a whaling ship to be out of oil
    • vacant
    • Inexperienced like a virgin
    • When sights whales the Jungfrau gets head start but Pequod still gets the kill of the whale
    • Pequod gained confidence
  • Gam 5- Rose-Bud
    • smells/rot
    • Ambergris comes out of their whale- although whale dead, value comes from decay
    • Stubb gets the whale from them (he says it is worthless but in reality it has the ambergris that they did not know abt)
    • Dead whale represents the Pequod and there is some redeemable qualities since he's not all the way there
    • He thinks about his wife and kid
    • Looks at sand from Nantucket-metaphor for longing for home
    • Still something redeemable in Ahab; he has not discarded all aspects of humanity yet
  • Gam 6- Samuel Enerby
    • Ship from London, captain Boomer lost arm to Moby Dick and has ivory arm like Ahab’s leg
    • Seen Moby Dick but don’t want to face him again - knows its not a great idea
    • Idea of foil: Boomer foil to Ahab - very different - went through similar experience
    • But Boomer just happy survive - able to let it go
    • Ahab wants revenge and has a one track mind
  • Gam 7- The Rachel
    • Gardiner (captain of Rachel) looking for his son who was lost at sea when his whaling ship encountered Moby Dick
    • Ahab refuses to help find him- reiterates Ahab valuing destiny over morality, dismissive and narcissistic, only wants vengeance
    • Rachel in bible is mother and loving figure
    • The Rachel saves Ishmael at the end of the novel
  • Gam 8- The Delight
    • Irony of the name Delight: the ship and much of its crew destroyed by Moby Dick
    • Coffin drops into water
    • Crew sees as sign of doom
    • Ahab ignores the sound so able to seek vengeance
